Data

Data Resource

I got data from Kaggle.

Variables

  • anime_id: myanimelist.net's unique id identifying an anime.
  • name: full name of anime.
  • genre: comma separated list of genres for this anime.
  • type: movie, TV, OVA, etc. OVA stands for Original Video Anime. ONA stands for Original Net Anime
  • episodes: how many episodes in this show. (1 if movie).
  • rating: average rating out of 10 for this anime.
  • members: number of community members that are in this anime's group

Anime Type Count

TV, OVA, and Movie categories ocupy more than 75% of the graph. Undoubtedly, TV is the most popular category. Derived anime from original anime called OVA is also popular due to the original anime. Usually the popular anime becomes movie series to extend the story, so these 3 categories are pretty much the same things.

Average Episodes of Each Anime Type

TV series average episodes is 33 episodes which is almost 3 seasons. However, OVA's average episodes is only 2.3 episodes. OVA are usually short series because it is not directly related to the original story. Meanwhile, ONA is a little bit longer than OVA. Music anime is not popular at all.

Scatterplot between Episodes and Rating (Interactice Graph)

This is a scatterplot graph to find out the relationship between episodes and rating. The total episodes of the popular TV series with over 8 rating tend to have less than 200 episodes. If the number of episodes go higher than 200, new users usually hesitate to watch a whole series. Also, there are weal positive correlation in all categories.
